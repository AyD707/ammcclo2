Vehicle Fluids

Fluids keep your vehicle running smoothly by reducing heat, lubricating parts, and ensuring systems operate properly. Checking fluid levels regularly helps prevent major mechanical issues.

Engine Oil

Engine oil lubricates internal engine components and prevents overheating. Low or dirty oil can cause serious engine damage.

  • Check oil using the dipstick
  • Change oil based on mileage or time interval
  • Use the correct oil type for your vehicle

Coolant

Coolant regulates engine temperature. Without proper coolant levels, engines can overheat quickly.

  • Check coolant in the reservoir tank
  • Never open the radiator when hot
  • Watch for leaks or overheating warnings

Brake Fluid

Brake fluid transfers force from the brake pedal to the braking system. Low fluid can reduce braking performance.

  • Check fluid level in the brake reservoir
  • Look for leaks near wheels or master cylinder
  • Replace fluid as recommended

Windshield Washer Fluid

Washer fluid keeps your windshield clean for better visibility. It is one of the easiest fluids to check and refill.

  • Fill washer reservoir when low
  • Use winter formula in cold climates
  • Keep extra fluid in your trunk
