Basic Maintenance

Routine maintenance helps your car last longer and prevents small issues from turning into expensive repairs. This page covers the most important maintenance habits for everyday drivers.

Oil Changes

Oil keeps your engine lubricated and helps reduce heat and wear. Driving with low or dirty oil can damage your engine over time.

  • Check your oil level regularly (especially before long trips)
  • Follow your owner’s manual for oil type and interval
  • Watch for warning lights and leaks

Brakes

Brakes are a safety system. If your car squeaks, grinds, vibrates while braking, or takes longer to stop, get it inspected.

  • Listen for squeaking or grinding
  • Pay attention to vibration while braking
  • Don’t ignore a brake warning light

Battery

Batteries weaken over time. If your engine cranks slowly, lights dim, or you need frequent jump starts, your battery may be failing.

  • Keep terminals clean (white/green corrosion = problem)
  • If your car sits often, consider a battery tender
  • Replace batteries that are old or unreliable

Filters

Filters keep contaminants out of your engine and cabin. Dirty filters reduce performance and air quality.

  • Engine air filter affects fuel efficiency
  • Cabin filter keeps interior air clean
  • Replace filters as recommended
