Tire Care

Tires affect safety, braking distance, and fuel efficiency. Checking pressure and tread regularly is one of the easiest ways to keep your car safe and your tires lasting longer.

Tire Pressure

Check tire pressure monthly and before long drives. The correct PSI is usually found on the driver’s door jamb sticker (not the tire sidewall).

  • Check pressure when tires are cold
  • Underinflated tires wear faster and reduce MPG
  • Overinflated tires can reduce traction

Tread Depth

Tread helps your tires grip the road, especially in rain. If tread is too low, your stopping distance increases.

  • Use the penny test (Lincoln’s head) for a quick check
  • Uneven wear can indicate alignment or suspension issues
  • Replace tires when tread is worn or damage is visible

Rotations and Alignments

Rotations help tires wear evenly. Alignments help your vehicle track straight and can prevent uneven wear.

  • Rotate tires every 5000–6000 miles
  • Get an alignment if the car pulls or the steering wheel is off-center
  • Uneven wear can shorten tire life significantly
